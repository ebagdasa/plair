About the Initiative

Pioneer Leaders in AI and Robotics (PLAIR) is an outreach program initiated by faculties from the Manning College of Information and Computer Sciences at UMass Amherst designed to address the challenges of highschool students who has limited access to research-oriented activities.

By connecting cutting-edge research in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics with high school education in Western Mass, we aim to inspire young minds through hands-on research opportunities and academic mentorship. Our initiative leverages the expertise of CICS researchers and graduate student mentors to create a dynamic pipeline for future STEM leaders while fostering lasting community partnerships.

Our Goals

Engage Students in STEM: Ignite curiosity and passion in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.
Hands-On Research: Provide real-world research projects guided by UMass CICS faculty and graduate student mentors.
Bridge Communities: Build lasting partnerships with Western Mass high schools.
Showcase Talent: Offer a platform for students to present their innovations.

Program Structure

Phase 1

Engaging introductory lecture at participating high schools

Phase 2

Competitive student selection process

Phase 3

Selected teams work on collaborative research projects

Phase 4

Showcase event at UMass Manning CICS.

Expected Outcomes & Impact

  • Educational Enrichment: High school students gain hands-on research experience that enhances critical thinking, problem-solving, and teamwork skills.
  • Pipeline to Higher Education: Participants develop strong connections with faculty and mentors from the Manning College of Information and Computer Sciences at UMass Amherst, encouraging them to pursue advanced studies and STEM careers.
  • Community Connection: The program fosters lasting partnerships between UMass Amherst and Western Mass high schools, strengthening community ties.
  • Demonstration of Excellence: A final showcase event provides a platform for students to present their innovative projects and celebrate their achievements.
