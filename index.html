Pioneer Leaders in AI (PLAIR)

An Initiative For Western Mass High School Students in STEM

About the Initiative

Pioneer Leaders in AI (PLAIR) is a pilot outreach program initiated by faculty from the Manning College of Information and Computer Sciences at UMass Amherst designed to address the challenges of a shrinking high school population in Massachusetts. By connecting cutting-edge research in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics with high school education in Western Mass, we aim to inspire young minds through hands-on research opportunities and academic mentorship. Our initiative leverages the expertise of CICS researchers and graduate student mentors to create a dynamic pipeline for future STEM leaders while fostering lasting community partnerships.

Our Goals

Engage Students in STEM: Ignite curiosity and passion in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.
Hands-On Research: Provide real-world research projects guided by faculty and graduate student mentors from the Manning College of Information and Computer Sciences at UMass Amherst.
Bridge Communities: Build lasting partnerships with Western Mass high schools.
Showcase Talent: Offer a platform for students to present their innovations.

Program Structure

The program begins with an engaging introductory lecture at participating high schools, followed by a competitive student selection process. Selected teams then work on collaborative research projects, culminating in a showcase event organized by CICS Professors.

Outcomes & Impact

  • Educational Enrichment: High school students gain hands-on research experience that enhances critical thinking, problem-solving, and teamwork skills.
  • Pipeline to Higher Education: Participants develop strong connections with faculty and mentors from the Manning College of Information and Computer Sciences at UMass Amherst, encouraging them to pursue advanced studies and STEM careers.
  • Community Connection: The program fosters lasting partnerships between UMass Amherst and Western Mass high schools, strengthening community ties.
  • Demonstration of Excellence: A final showcase event provides a platform for students to present their innovative projects and celebrate their achievements.
